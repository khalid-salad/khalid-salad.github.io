LeetCode is terrible, from the way it encourages this moronic culture of whiteboarding to the outright
*awful* solutions they post. See my page [on describing algorithms](../../blog/how-to-explain-an-algorithm).

In the interest of putting a dent in this, as well as providing a useful public good, I will write up solutions to
LeetCode exercises, with technical explanations on correctness and runtime where appropriate. The solutions are in
Python because I can just indent my pseudocode and it usually passes the test cases.
