My Commitment to Not Writing like ****

Curse Words

I will censor all the cursewords for now. I will try and find a toggle.

One of the worst things about reading stuff from the internet is this expectation that, because you are probably reading this in a web browser, it is cool to add stupid distractions like footnotes1 and urls to unrelated bull****.

Did you know that the modern web is a great way to train your neck muscles?

Notice, for example, that simply linking to something gives an unintended emphasis. At best, this is distracting, but in many cases it changes the tone of the text.

You might have thought “the next paragraph will continue the topic being discussed”, but you would be wrong. The current best practice is to distract your readers so much that they can’t follow your writing, then have them eviscerate you in your evaluations and on ratemyprofessors.

Similarly, the mere presence of a footnote can be distracting. For example, try and read the rest of this paragraph, but disregard the upcoming footnote. I am not a big fan of Lorem Ipsum, because it just makes me feel insecure about the fact that the only Latin I know is “post hoc ergo propter hoc”2 which roughly translates to “I studied Spanish in high school.”3

With that in mind, I approach my writing with:

  1. A considerate approach to distractions. If the material you are serving is meant to be read, then it should, disregarding markup, be mostly plaintext.
  2. A commitment to accessibility. The tabi theme used for this site is accessible. Currently, math and diagrams are rendered clientside using KaTeX and Mermaid. I hope to eventually have everything rendered serverside (with something like MathJax, which has a reputation for accessibility). The ideal would be to have the website fully functional with no JavaScript.
  3. Respect for the reader. External links will be kept to a minimum and clearly indicated (for example, here is an external link to Daniel Podgorski’s excellent Unchosen Undead: A Thorough Existentialist Philosophical Analysis of FromSoftware’s Original Dark Souls).
